BACKGROUND OF THE AUTHOR


Karl Gabrielle De Los Santos is a Filipino writer from Cavite City. He is a graduate of the University of Santo Tomas, where he majored in Creative Writing. De Los Santos has won several awards for his writing, including the Palanca Award for Literature and the National Book Award. He is the author of the short story collection "Hindi Ko Alam" and the novel "All My Lonely Islands." INSPIRATION


The author, De Los Santos, drew inspiration from his own experiences as a young person. He too grappled with periods of bewilderment and uncertainty, unsure of the trajectory of his life. His intent was to craft a narrative that would furnish solace and encouragement to youth undergoing similar trials. He desired to convey that it's perfectly acceptable to be muddled and to falter along the way. The overarching message is to persevere steadfastly and never relinquish the pursuit of one's dreams. And the people who want to learn even if they have reading difficulties, He wrote this story because he wanted to show the world or to show the universe rather that we can learn something as long as we don’t lose hope.


SUMMARY


The story “Hindi ko alam” is made for people who have reading issues. We Filipinos only think about our own interest but the most important thing is to keep trying and never give up. The narrative revolves around a youthful protagonist grappling with the search for her purpose in the world. She's besieged by uncertainty regarding her life's direction and haunted by the fear of committing erroneous decisions. Furthermore, she senses that she doesn't quite fit in among her school peers. One fateful day, her path crosses with an elderly woman. The old woman tells the girl that she is special, and that she has a lot to offer the world. She also tells the girl that it's okay not to know everything right away. It's okay to be confused and to make mistakes.The most important thing is to keep trying and to never give up.The girl is inspired by the old woman's words. She realizes that it's okay not to know everything. She doesn't have to have all the answers right away. She just needs to keep trying and to never give up on her dreams. Reading can lead to a deeper understanding of ideas and a more intelligent and insightful society. Reading stories, especially those written in our language, can serve as a companion to understanding ideas, and can help promote unity among Filipinos.


CHARACTER LIMELIGHT


The story's central figure is a young girl ensnared in the quest for her place in the world. Her life path remains elusive, and she grapples with the anxiety of making incorrect choices. Additionally, she feels like an outsider in her school environment. This character is highly relatable, as many adolescents navigate a phase of feeling adrift and bewildered. They confront the quandary of life's purpose and fear the repercussions of missteps. The importance of reading, especially in the context of our language, is highlighted. Karl Gabrielle De Los Santos aims to show the importance of reading to readers in order to promote unity in the country.


LESSON


Books are totally useless unless you take their advice. If you just keep reading them, thinking.. "Ohh that's so insightful! that changes everything," but never actually doing anything different, then pretty quickly the feeling will wear off and you'll start searching for another book to fill the void. Do not fear failure but rather fear not trying. The embrace of confusion and the acceptance of errors are part and parcel of the human experience. The paramount message is to persist unyieldingly and steadfastly nurture one's dreams.
